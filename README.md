# Jedi Knight 4 Fun - A Roleplaying Initiative

Jedi Knight 4 Fun (a.k.a. JK4F and JKFF) is a fanmade project based on Jedi Academy, inspired by FiveM and RedM.

Roleplaying in GTA V and Red Dead Redemption 2 tends to be structured and predictable. In FiveM, you have a list of whitelisted jobs, and you have access to a massive world map. Furthermore, certain FiveM servers choose their own interior designs for specific buildings via mods and plugins, depending on their preferences; they implement their own character customization and clothing options, vehicles, food items, and more.

The ultimate mission of Jedi Knight 4 Fun is to attempt to mirror the appeal of FiveM (and by extension RedM): to enable immersive roleplaying experiences within the Star Wars Expanded Universe by utilizing Jedi Academy's fantastical setting and unmatched lightsaber combat to allow for unique and exciting gameplay; in-game voice chatting based on proximity, slightly upscaled textures, graphical improvements, smoother gameplay compared to baseJKA, native support for RGB sabers and clothes, and more.

Server hosts are able to define their own local settings, as well as custom lore-related rules - including whether saber combat is enabled, how frequently you can upgrade your force powers, etc.

If you would like to follow the progress of Jedi Knight 4 Fun, feel free to bookmark, star, and/or watch my repository!

## Enhanced Gameplay Mechanics

I have been playing Jedi Academy since early childhood. Because of this, I have had over a decade of dedicated gameplay to notice the egregious discrepancies between Singleplayer and Multiplayer mechanics.

This is understandable - Jedi Academy was released in 2003, which means that the original developers put strenuous effort into ensuring that Multiplayer combat felt responsive and non-demanding for slow Internet connections.

Working on JK4F in 2026 (and beyond) grants me a massive advantage: modern-day gaming computers are what 2003 developers would have called "the stuff of dreams" - gigabytes of RAM and VRAM, powerful CPUs, fast SSDs, and hundreds of megabits per second in Internet connection, if not gigabits. A lot of the restrictions put in place to preserve bandwidth can be loosened or entirely discarded, leveraging modern technology.

Some of the Multiplayer combat rules were restrictive in of themselves, independent from Internet connectivity. I personally thought some of them were complete bullshit, so I lifted some of these restrictions.

Combined with certain gameplay mechanic reworks, such as remastered saber throwing, dual Absorb-Protect, and animations that are more similar to those in Singleplayer, I have bridged the gap between Multiplayer and Singleplayer to a remarkable degree. In simple terms, Jedi Academy Multiplayer now plays much more similarly to our beloved Jedi Academy Singleplayer.

Going into this project having experience with platformer games that are notoriously butter-smooth to play, such as Celeste, Jedi Knight 4 Fun's physics were modified for ease of use - air acceleration is faster than air friction (was lower before), which unlocks higher-precision control over your character's movement. This is unprecedented for Jedi Academy, because while the game is a paragon of spectacular lightsaber combat mechanics, falling to your death because you accidentally executed that one specific jump that locks you in the air used to be uncomfortably common.

# Jedi Knight 4 Fun is a fork of OpenJK

The [OpenJK repository](https://github.com/JACoders/OpenJK) outlines:

> OpenJK is a community effort to maintain and improve the game and engine powering Jedi Academy and Jedi Outcast, while maintaining _full backwards compatibility_ with the existing games and mods.  
> This project does not intend to add major features, rebalance, or otherwise modify core gameplay.
> 
> Our aims are to:
> 
> - Improve the stability of the engine by fixing bugs and improving performance.
> - Support more hardware (x86_64, Arm, Apple Silicon) and software platforms (Linux, macOS)
> - Provide a clean base from which new code modifications can be made.

## Installing JK4F

To install Jedi Knight 4 Fun, you will first need Jedi Academy installed. If you don't already own the game, you can buy it from online stores, such as [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/6020/), [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Star-Wars-Jedi-Knight-Academy-Pc/dp/B0000A2MCN), or [GOG](https://www.gog.com/game/star_wars_jedi_knight_jedi_academy).

## JK4F Compatibility

While the original OpenJK repository claims to be compatible with Windows, Linux, and macOS, as the developer of a heavily modified version of Jedi Academy, I cannot guarantee full compatibility across all major operating systems. My developer environment utilizes Windows 11.

Despite this, feel free to follow [the original instructions](https://github.com/JACoders/OpenJK#for-players) and see how it goes.
